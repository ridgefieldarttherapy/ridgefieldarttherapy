ART THERAPY IS...

... an integrative, holistic, evidence-based practice that uses applied psychological theory, the creative process, and active art-creation to ensure a safe space. I provide a person-centered approach to therapy to work with you, where you are at. My practice stems from the visual arts and focuses on the process, not the product. Your treatment plan will be a mixture of mindfulness, cognitive behavioral exercises, art therapy, and talk therapy to determine individual goals, increase motivation and resilience.

ART THERAPY COMES IN MANY FORMS...

The therapeutic process takes shape in individual and group formats, using a range of client-appropriate materials, from acrylic, pencils, graphic markers, charcoal, pastel, collage, plaster, you name it. You don't need to be an artist, or possess specific skills to participate and benefit from the process. Art therapy provides an opportunity for self-discovery, healing and exploration.

Therapeutic art-making provides distance, personal reflection, participant motivation through action, encourages feedback and self-examination, and creates a safe space to cultivate the psychotherapeutic relationship.
